Welcome! I am a researcher in physics working at the intersection of market microstructure, high-frequency data, and topological data analysis.

I am a PhD researcher in the Department of Physics at the National Institute of Technology Sikkim, where I recently submitted my doctoral thesis in econophysics. I use Markov modelling, time-series methods and tools from physics to understand how prices and orders evolve inside financial markets.

Markets, data, topology

Financial markets generate enormous, fast-moving data, but their underlying dynamics are often hidden behind noise. My work treats order flow as a stochastic system and brings methods from physics and applied mathematics to reveal structure in how limit orders and prices change over the trading day.
