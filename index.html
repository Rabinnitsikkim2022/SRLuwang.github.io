Salam Rabindrajit Luwang

Welcome! I am a researcher in physics working at the intersection of market microstructure, high-frequency data, and topological data analysis. I use Markov modelling, time-series methods and tools from physics to understand how prices and orders evolve inside financial markets.

I am a PhD researcher in the Department of Physics at the National Institute of Technology Sikkim, where I recently submitted my doctoral thesis in econophysics.

SL

Markets, data, topology

Financial markets generate enormous, fast-moving data, but their underlying dynamics are often hidden behind noise. My work treats order flow as a stochastic system and applies methods from physics and applied mathematics, such as discrete-time Markov chains and topological data analysis, to reveal structure in how limit orders and prices change over the trading day.

Research

My main interests are market microstructure and the data-driven analysis of high-frequency, tick-by-tick financial data. Recurring themes in my work include:

intraday order-flow and limit-order book dynamics · discrete-time Markov chain modelling · time-series analysis · topological data analysis and persistent homology · econophysics of market crises.
