Research

My main interests are market microstructure and the data-driven analysis of high-frequency, tick-by-tick financial data. I model order flow as a stochastic process and apply methods from physics and applied mathematics, such as discrete-time Markov chains and topological data analysis, to uncover structure in how limit orders and prices evolve over the trading day.

A recurring thread in my work is the behaviour of markets during periods of stress, such as trade wars and pandemic-driven crashes, where I study how order dynamics and the topological structure of multiple markets change.

Topics I work on

Market microstructure High-frequency / order-flow data Limit-order book dynamics Markov chains Time-series analysis Topological data analysis Econophysics
